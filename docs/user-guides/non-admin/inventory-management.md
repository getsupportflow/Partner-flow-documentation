# Inventory Management — Service Provider

Inventory in PartnerFlow is dynamic and designed for collaboration between service creators and providers. When a service is shared, both parties gain visibility into the stock levels linked to that service — giving creators confidence in provider readiness while ensuring providers can manage the parts they need to deliver effectively.

## Products

The **Products** section shows all items held in stock that are associated with a service. You can view current stock levels for every part linked to the services you are executing.

Stock levels change only through controlled, auditable events:

| Event | Description |
|---|---|
| **Items received** | New stock is received into inventory |
| **Items consumed** | Parts are used during a service and logged as consumed |
| **Damaged items** | Items signed off as damaged with photo evidence attached |
| **Stock review** | A stock review is requested by the creator and signed off by both parties |

Service creators can view your inventory but cannot change it directly. This maintains security and independence while giving creators the visibility they need to plan.

## Purchase Orders

When stock falls below the minimum threshold configured by the service creator, PartnerFlow can:

- **Automatically draft a purchase order** ready for your review
- Allow you to **raise a manual purchase order** at any time

### Managing Purchase Orders

1. Go to **Inventory → Purchase Orders**
2. View all open, submitted, and fulfilled orders
3. Click **New Order** to raise a manual replenishment request
4. Track order progress from submission through to fulfilment

Purchase orders ensure stock is replenished in time to keep services moving without delays.

## Benefits

| Benefit | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Transparency** | Service creators have a clear view of your stock linked to their services |
| **Security** | You retain full control of your own inventory — creators can monitor but not modify |
| **Continuity** | Automated draft purchase orders reduce the risk of shortages and service delays |
| **Collaboration** | Shared visibility ensures both sides stay aligned on resource availability |
