# Contracts

Contracts formalise the terms of your services and align expectations between you, your partners, and customers. PartnerFlow extracts all relevant details from your service flow — stages, transitions, service options, and documentation — and generates a draft contract automatically.

## Contract Types

| Type | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **SLA (Service Level Agreement)** | Defines performance standards, timelines, and response expectations |
| **SOW (Statement of Work)** | Outlines the scope of work, deliverables, and responsibilities for both parties |
| **MSA (Master Service Agreement)** | Establishes overarching terms that govern long-term partnerships |

## Generating a Contract

1. Open the **Contract** tab in the service editor
2. Select the contract type
3. PartnerFlow generates a draft based on your flow configuration
4. Review the draft — all stage names, SLA thresholds, pricing rules, and service options are pre-filled

## Review and Sign-off

Drafted contracts can be shared internally with your legal team or external solicitors for review before presenting them to partners.

Once approved:
1. Share the contract with the prospective service provider
2. The provider can view the associated stages, transitions, and documentation — they see the full scope before deciding to accept
3. The provider signs digitally within PartnerFlow (e-signature flow)
4. A secondary verification step is sent to the provider's registered email for compliance
5. The service creator countersigns to finalise the agreement

## Ongoing Reference and Updates

All signed contracts are stored securely within PartnerFlow. You can:

- Revisit them at any time for reference or training
- Issue updated versions when service terms change
- Route amendments through the same approval and signature workflow before they become active

## Benefits

| Benefit | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Clarity** | Both parties have a precise understanding of deliverables, responsibilities, and expectations from day one |
| **Compliance** | Services are delivered within agreed legal and operational frameworks |
| **Trust** | Accountability and transparency strengthen the relationship with partners |
| **Efficiency** | Contract creation is integrated directly into the service setup — no separate tooling required |
| **Continuity** | A complete history of contracts, approvals, and updates is maintained to avoid confusion in long-term partnerships |
