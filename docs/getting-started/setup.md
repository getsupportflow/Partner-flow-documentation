# Setup Guide

Before creating your first service, complete three foundational steps: set up your company profile, add your team members, and connect your external systems.

## Step 1 — Company Profile

Navigate to **Settings → Company Profile** to configure your organisation's details.

We recommend filling in all of the following before going further:

- **Company name and logo** — your logo appears on emails, contracts, and the customer portal
- **Billing address** — used for invoice generation
- **Shipping address** — used as the default logistics origin
- **Contact details** — phone, support email, and website

Your logo and contact details are embedded in auto-generated contracts, so having these set up first ensures everything looks professional from day one.

## Step 2 — Add Users

Navigate to **Settings → Users** (or go directly to `https://portal.warrantyflow.io/en/settings/users`) to add the team members who will use PartnerFlow.

1. Click **Create New User**
2. Enter the user's name and email address
3. Assign a role (Management, Support, Technician, or Admin — or a custom role you have defined)
4. Save — the user receives an invitation email with login instructions

Roles control what each user can see and do within the platform. See [User Management](/docs/user-guides/admin/user-management) for a full breakdown of permissions.

## Step 3 — Connect Integrations

Navigate to **Settings → Connections** (`https://portal.warrantyflow.io/en/settings/connections`) to integrate PartnerFlow with your other systems.

### Recommended Connections

| Integration | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Email (Gmail)** | Send automated notifications and receive customer replies |
| **Logistics Provider** | Generate shipping orders and track deliveries |
| **Automation (n8n)** | Trigger custom automations on service transitions |

### Additional Integrations

You can also connect CRM systems, accounting platforms, ecommerce tools, and more from the Connections page.

Integrations are optional at setup — you can connect them at any time — but connecting email before creating your first service means automated notifications work immediately when a record is created.

With your company profile set, users added, and key integrations connected, you are ready to create your first service.
